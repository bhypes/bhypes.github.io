My name is Brooke Hypes. I am currently a third-year PhD student in Planning, Governance, and Globalization at Virginia Tech. I position my work within critical governance studies with particular focus on Appalachia.

What does that mean, you ask?

Tautologically—I take a critical perspective on policy and governance.

In practice, this means I approach policy and governance as sites where power operates through knowledge claims rather than neutral technical systems.

Why Appalachia?

Appalachia matters. The region is often reduced to stereotypes of poverty and coalfields, but it is also a site of vibrant community and democratic possibility.

My work examines how governance structures shape whose knowledge is treated as credible in Appalachian communities.

But who are you, really?

I grew up near Charleston, West Virginia, spending Sundays in Fayette County with my grandparents, where food, music, and family shaped how I understand the world.

Why I teach

Teaching, for me, is about co-creation. Students are not passive recipients of knowledge but active participants in its production.

What I teach

I teach practical ethics, critical thinking, and political science courses at BridgeValley Community & Technical College and Virginia Tech.

How I teach

I emphasize dialogue, writing-as-thinking, and structured reflection, including engagement with AI as a learning tool.
